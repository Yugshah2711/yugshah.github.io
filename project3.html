Experimental Investigations and Effect of Nano-Powder-Mixed EDM Variables on Performance Measures of Nitinol SMA

Published in: Journal of Manufacturing and Materials Processing

DOI: 10.3390/jmmp060131

Project Overview

This study investigates the effect of adding expanded graphite (EG) nano-powder in the wire electrical discharge machining (WEDM) process for machining the Ti6Al4V alloy. The Ti6Al4V alloy, widely used in aerospace and biomedical applications, is challenging to machine due to poor thermal conductivity and tool wear. The research explores how vital machining parameters, such as pulse-on time (Ton), pulse-off time (Toff), and current, influence critical performance measures like material removal rate (MRR) and surface roughness (SR). The incorporation of EG nano-powder enhanced the machining process by improving spark discharge efficiency and particle flushing, resulting in improved MRR and reduced SR. Additionally, the study used Taguchi’s L9 design for experimental trials and employed ANOVA to validate the regression models. This research offers optimized machining conditions for improved productivity and surface integrity.

Key Contributions

- Achieved up to 45.35% improvement in MRR and a 36.16% reduction in SR using EG nano-powder.
- Developed regression models and validated them through ANOVA analysis.
- Utilized scanning electron microscopy (SEM) to analyze and verify enhanced surface morphology.
- Conducted multi-response optimization using the Heat Transfer Search (HTS) algorithm to balance MRR and SR.
- The study demonstrated that expanded graphite can significantly enhance WEDM operations by reducing surface defects and improving machining performance.

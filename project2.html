Project 2 - Experimental Investigations and Effect of Nano-Powder-Mixed EDM Variables

Published in: Journal of Manufacturing and Materials Processing

DOI: 10.3390/jmmp060131

Project Overview

This research focused on enhancing the machining performance of Nitinol SMA using alumina (Al₂O₃) nano-powder mixed in the dielectric fluid during electrical discharge machining (EDM). Nitinol’s shape-memory and superelastic properties make it essential for biomedical and aerospace applications, but machining it poses challenges due to surface defects and tool wear. Key machining parameters such as pulse-on time, pulse-off time, current, and powder concentration (PC) were varied to assess their impact on material removal rate (MRR), surface roughness (SR), and tool wear rate (TWR). The introduction of alumina nano-powder improved spark efficiency and debris flushing, leading to enhanced machining outcomes. Optimized parameters resulted in higher MRR, smoother surfaces, and reduced tool wear, making the process more efficient. These improvements are particularly valuable for applications requiring precision machining with minimal surface damage, such as stents and surgical tools.

Key Contributions

- Achieved optimal parametric settings through the Teaching-Learning-Based Optimization (TLBO) technique.
- Improved MRR to 43.57 mg/min and reduced SR to 3.73 µm with optimized parameters.
- Utilized scanning electron microscopy (SEM) to analyze and validate the enhanced surface morphology.
- Developed regression models and ANOVA to identify significant variables and interactions for optimization.

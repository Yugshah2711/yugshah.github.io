Optimizing Life’s Blueprint: Guiding DNA Replication through Modeling

This project was the result of the course I took, CME 209: Mathematical Modeling of Biological Systems.

Project Overview

This project focuses on modeling DNA replication under varying environmental conditions such as temperature, pH levels, and nutrient availability. DNA replication, a fundamental biological process, is highly sensitive to these external factors, which can influence the speed, accuracy, and overall success of the replication process.

Through the development of mathematical models and simulations, this study explores how these environmental variations impact key replication parameters such as initiation timing, elongation rates, and replication fork stability. Special attention is given to the potential increase in error rates and replication delays that may arise from environmental stress.

The goal is to predict the behavior of replication machinery under adverse conditions and identify optimal environmental ranges for efficient DNA replication. These insights are critical not only for experimental biology but also for applications in synthetic biology and biotechnology, where maintaining precise replication is essential for producing reliable and reproducible results.

Key Contributions

- Developed mathematical models to simulate DNA replication under various environmental conditions.
- Analyzed the impact of temperature, pH, and nutrient levels on replication accuracy and speed.
- Identified environmental stressors that lead to increased replication errors and delays.
- Proposed optimal environmental conditions to ensure efficient DNA replication for biotechnology applications.
- Offered insights into replication behavior for synthetic biology, enhancing reproducibility in experimental settings.

Figure 1: Mean Temperature vs. DNA Replication Fork Rate

This plot illustrates the relationship between the mean temperature and the DNA replication fork rate. The trend shows how the rate increases as the temperature rises, indicating the optimal thermal environment for efficient DNA replication. However, the plot also highlights a plateau, suggesting the saturation point where further increases in temperature do not significantly enhance the fork rate.

Figure 2: Mean Temperature vs. Total Replicated DNA Pairs

This graph demonstrates the correlation between mean temperature and the total number of replicated DNA pairs. The data reveal that as the temperature increases, the replication efficiency improves up to a certain point, beyond which further temperature rise has minimal impact. This behavior emphasizes the importance of maintaining optimal conditions for maximizing DNA replication efficiency, critical for understanding biological processes under varying environmental conditions.

Figure 3: Mean pH vs. DNA Replication Fork Rate

This graph displays the relationship between mean pH levels and the DNA replication fork rate. It shows that as the pH increases from 6.5 to around 8.0, there is a corresponding increase in the replication fork rate. Beyond this optimal pH range, the replication rate plateaus, suggesting that highly alkaline conditions may not significantly enhance replication. This information underscores the importance of maintaining proper pH levels in biological experiments to ensure efficient DNA replication.

Figure 4: Mean pH vs. Total Replicated DNA Pairs

This plot illustrates the correlation between mean pH and the total number of replicated DNA pairs. The data indicates that optimal pH levels facilitate greater DNA replication, reaching a peak around pH 7.5 to 8.0, where the total replicated DNA pairs increase significantly. Beyond this point, the rate of replication begins to stabilize, suggesting that too high or too low pH may hinder effective replication. This figure emphasizes the necessity of optimizing pH conditions in experimental designs focused on DNA synthesis and replication efficiency.
